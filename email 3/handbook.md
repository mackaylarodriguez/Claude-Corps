## The communities we serve

We cover three counties: **Harlan, Price, Ellis**, through 47 partner agencies (church pantries, school programs, shelters, senior centers, community kitchens). Roughly 30% of the families we serve are primarily Spanish-speaking, concentrated in Ellis County and parts of Price — and Ellis's Spanish-speaking population has been the fastest-growing segment we serve for two years running. Anything we send to a wide audience — flyers, intake forms, signage, autoresponses — should work in both English and Spanish.

A handful of our partners function as **hubs** — they run their own distribution day *and* forward part of what we send them to two or three smaller satellite pantries nearby that we don't deliver to directly. We don't track the forwarding formally; it's worth knowing that what shows up at a hub reaches more households than its own roster suggests.

## Donation policies

**Corporate / pallet-scale donations.** All routes through Marcus. **No one but Marcus confirms dock space, delivery windows, or whether we accept short-dated product.** Even when we have the space and would clearly take it, accepting on his behalf has caused real problems — a draft confirmation went out last fall promising a delivery slot we couldn't honor; the donor pulled out and we lost the relationship.

**Individual donations.** Drop-off only, during public hours (above). What we can accept:

- ✅ Sealed, unexpired, non-perishable food (canned goods, pasta, rice, cereal, shelf-stable proteins, baby formula in original packaging)
- ❌ Homemade items, opened packages, expired items, anything past best-by
- ❌ Toiletries, clothing, household items (we don't have the space; redirect to Goodwill or local shelters)
- ❌ Refrigerated/frozen donations from individuals (we can't verify cold chain)

**Our current "most needed" list** lives in `Drive / Operations / most_needed.docx` — Priya updates it monthly. If a donor asks for it, send the latest version, don't recite from memory.

## Partner agency operations

- Each agency has a standard monthly allocation (lbs) set quarterly by Marcus + Diane based on families served. Roster lives in Airtable.
- **Off-cycle / extra allocation requests:** must be reviewed by Marcus against current inventory before any commitment is made. We have promised allocations we couldn't deliver in the past — last fall's incident with First Baptist Pantry escalated to the board. **Never confirm an allocation in a draft reply.**
- **Delivery issues** (missing item, damaged item, wrong drop, expired product): log the agency name, date, and what was wrong in `#warehouse` Slack so Marcus can check the truck manifest. Treat any quality or food-safety flag as time-sensitive — Marcus needs to see it before the next truck loads. Don't promise a redelivery without his sign-off.
- Most allocation conversations should ultimately move from email to a quick call — partners prefer it and so does Marcus.
- **Routes:** Ellis is the longest run from the warehouse (~50 min each way). When a truck's running tight on time or weight, the Ellis stops tend to be the ones that get short-loaded. 
- **Fleet:** three box trucks — T-12, T-14, T-21. There's also a `T-99` slot in the inventory system that the warehouse uses to test the scale and barcode reader at month-start; those rows show up in exports but aren't real deliveries.

## Food assistance seekers

- **We do not gatekeep.** Never ask for proof of income, immigration status, household documents, or anything else before pointing someone at food.
- **First step is always:** connect them with their nearest partner pantry. We have a public lookup on our website (`riverbendfood.org/find-food`) — give them the link and ask for their ZIP if they haven't shared one.
- For acute need (no food today, recently displaced, kids hungry now), the intake line (555-0117) gets them to a person who can place a same-day call to a partner.
- Reply in whatever language the sender used. Most non-English mail is Spanish; we have a 1-page intake handout in Spanish — `Drive / Programs / intake_es.pdf`.

## Volunteer program (Priya)

- All volunteers complete a one-page **liability waiver** before their first shift. New volunteers sign on arrival; we don't require it pre-signed online (yet).
- **Minimum age:**
  - Warehouse sort: 14+ with parental consent, 16+ unaccompanied
  - Mobile pantry routes: **18+ only** (no minors on routes — insurance constraint)
  - Public drop-off coverage: 16+ unaccompanied
- **Background check** required only for: regular partner-pantry liaison roles, and shifts that involve direct work with children (e.g., school-distribution events). Not required for warehouse sort or single mobile-pantry days.
- **Current confirmation/reminder workflow** (this is the part Priya wants to rebuild — see her email): she manually texts every confirmed volunteer the day before the shift. Between the form, the sheet, and the texts it's most of her Mondays and a chunk of Thursday — call it six hours a week. There is no automated reminder system. No-show rate is anecdotally 30–40%; we don't track it formally.
- **Group sign-ups** (schools, NHS, corporate): coordinate by email with Priya — we cap groups at the listed shift size and don't always combine groups across organizations.
- **Cancellations:** weather-related cancellations are decided by Marcus by 6 AM the day-of and pushed to volunteers via group text and a banner on the volunteer page (the banner doesn't actually exist yet — it's been on Priya's wishlist for a year).
